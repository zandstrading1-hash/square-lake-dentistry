When Should Children First Visit the Dentist?

Earlier than most parents expect. Here’s the simple rule of thumb, and how to make that first visit a happy one.

It’s one of the most common questions new parents ask — and the answer surprises a lot of people: your child should see a dentist earlier than you might think.

The simple rule of thumb

Most dentists recommend a first visit by your child’s first birthday, or within six months of their first tooth appearing — whichever comes first. Starting early helps in two ways: it lets us catch any concerns while they’re tiny, and it helps your child get comfortable with the dentist before any treatment is ever needed.

What happens at the first visit

Early visits are short and gentle. We’ll gently check your child’s teeth and gums, talk through teething and home care, and answer your questions about brushing, diet and habits like thumb-sucking. Mostly, it’s about making the chair a friendly, no-pressure place.

Helping your child feel at ease

  • Keep your own tone upbeat — children take their cues from you.
  • Book a time when your child is usually well-rested and fed.
  • Bring a favorite comfort toy if it helps.
  • Let us do the explaining; we use kid-friendly language and plenty of encouragement.

Building habits that last

Brush twice a day with a smear of fluoride toothpaste as soon as that first tooth appears, and make it part of the routine. Those early habits — and early visits — set the foundation for a lifetime of healthy smiles.
